Online Car Auctions in North America

This page will be analysing and exploring the prices of cars for online auctions in the US, the occurences of certain characteristics overtime and take a closer look into the top colours and car brands.

Analysing Prices

The map of North America above displays the mean car prices sold in each state from 1973 to 2020. The state Kentucky has the highest average auction price of $44969.44, a surprising statistic for a relatively small state. In contrast, Montana has the lowest average car auction price of $2825.00 and Wyoming has sold one car for free ($0) in an online auction. Hence, the state with the highest prices cars on average is Kentucky.


The boxplot displays the distribution of car prices by their respective brands from the year 2000 to 2020.

The boxplot can be used to identify car brands within your desired price range.

The brands with the largest price range amongst the cars are Ford, Mercedes-benz and Dodge.

The brands with the highest medians are a Lexus and BMW, and they should be considered if you are looking for an expensive car.

Heartlands would be an ideal brand for those looking for a cheaper car, with a small and low cost range.




Occurences over time

The multi-series chart on the right displays the trends of mean car prices over time from 2000 to 2020.

It can instantly be seen that BMW and Lexus are following increasing trends in prices compared to the other brands.

Mercedes-benz makes a very sharp increase in price in 2017, however falls sharply the following year, showing unsteady fluctuations in prices.

Dodge also sells at unstable prices, however after 2014 the prices become more stable and maintain an average price above $10,000.

If you are looking for a car that is increasing in value, BMW and Lexus would be ideal brands.

The annotated brands are the only car brands that have been sold in 2020 (latest update).



Click on the legend circles to see the points more clearly.

The scatterplot on the left is used to show the relationship of miles travelled by a car with its price over time.

As time goes by, there are more cars being sold online in the US, with majority of the cars being clean vehicles .

The relationship observed is the further a car has travelled (in miles), the lower its price.

Cars with a status of salvage insurance typically sell for a lower price than clean vehicles .

The maximum price for a salvage vehicle was $20,000 in 2012.

Looking closer into popular attributes

Black, silver, gray, red, and blue, are some of the most popular car colours. The stacked bar chart above displays the proportions of these colours for the top 5 brands. Black is the most popular colour in Ford, Chervrolet and Nissan but it is the 2nd most popular in Dodge and BMW. Majority of BMW cars are sold in blue , unlike the other colours with less popularity in blue . Red is the least popular out of the 4, and there have been no BMW's sold in red . Even though silver and gray are similar colours, gray is more popular. This chart suggests that darker colours tend to be popular with cars such as black and gray .

Salvage insurance vehicles travel for longer distances than clean vehicles on average. This indicates that cars that have travelled for a shorter distance are more likely to be cleaner and in better condition. It is recommended to purchase a clean vehicle if you wish to have a newer car. The top 5 states with the highest sales can be selected to view the status' of these brands. Kentucky and Illinois have sold cars with both status', but not the other three states. Hence, cars from Ontario, New Mexico and Washington are likey to be clean vehicles .
