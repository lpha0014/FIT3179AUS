Online Car Auctions in North America

Analysing and exploring the prices of cars for online auctions in the US, the occurences of certain characteristics overtime and a closer look into the top colours and car brands.

Analysing Prices

The map of North America above displays the mean car prices sold in each state from 1973 to 2020. The state Kentucky has the highest average auction price of $44969.44, a surprising statistic for a relatively small state. In contrast, Montana has the lowest average car auction price of $2825.00 and Wyoming has sold one car for free ($0) in an online auction. Hence, the state with the highest prices cars on average is Kentucky.


The boxplot above displays the distribution of car prices by their respective brands from the year 2000 to 2020. The brands with the largest price range amongst the cars are audi, bmw and lexus (not including outliers). The maximum price of a car was a Mercedes Benz with an auctioned price of $84,900. The brands with the highest medians are a Lexus and BMW, and they should be considered if you are looking for an expensive car. Heartlands would be an ideal brand for those looking for a cheaper car, with a small and low cost range.



Occurences over time

The heat map on the right shows the number of car sales from the year 2000.

It can instantly be seen that Ford is the car brand that makes the most sales since 2000 and sells the most cars in 2019 with a count of 476 cars.

It can also be seen that many of the cars sell around 1 car a year such as Acura and Toyota.

The drop-down selection can be used to see the count of the top 5 colours over time. The maximum count of black cars is 97 in 2019 from Ford.

Chevrolet also sells relatively consistently over the years across all 5 colours

Ford sells the highest number of cars across all 5 colours in 2019, and is indeed the most popular car to be sold online.



Click on the legend circles to see the points more clearly.

The scatterplot on the left is used to show the relationship of miles travelled by a car with its price over time.

As time goes by, there are more cars being sold online in the US, with majority of the cars being clean vehciles.

The relationship observed is the further a car has travelled (in miles), the lower its price.

Cars with a status of 'salvage insurance' typically sell for a lower price than clean vehicles.

The maximum price for a salvage vehicle was $20,000 in 2012.

The status of the top 5 car brands are visualised above, and vehciles with the status of 'salvage insurance' travelled for longer dustances than clean vehciles.
